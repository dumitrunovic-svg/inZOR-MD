Electronic Gaps Near Quasi-Degeneracy: Minimal H2 Diagnostic, Ethylene Exploration in inZOR-ND, and the Discrete-Search Benchmark Boundary

--- arXiv abstract (paste; LaTeX $...$ allowed on arXiv) ---
Near quasi-degeneracy, multi-reference methods depend critically on the active space. Poor orbital choices can distort excitation gaps and state orderings long before a full potential-energy surface is trusted. This note unifies two complementary inZOR-ND threads under the same chemical motif---gaps under electronic stress---while keeping their algorithms and claims distinct. First, we document a minimal H2 diagnostic: a seven-point H--H stretch (cc-pVDZ) with SA-CASSCF(2,2) HOMO--LUMO gaps comparing active MO indices chosen by inZOR-ND to a NOON-style rule. The inZOR-ND track yields a monotone gap curve across the scan; the NOON-tracked pair does not, with a documented spike at R=2.0 Angstrom (~18.14 eV) versus ~2 eV at neighbouring points. Second, we summarise ethylene near torsional quasi-degeneracy in the continuous exploration stack: SA-CASSCF(2,2) gap-style observables feed an evolving population (2D/3D, multiple seeds, score variants) in the dedicated scene scene_ethylene_quasidegen. We reference exported validation figures and a separate 3D regional technical report on multi-basin low-gap structure, discovery speed versus uniform control, and reproducibility limits for identical probe traces. We state claim hygiene: continuous population logs must not be cited as discrete orbital-set search benchmark outcomes. Tabulated H2 numbers are frozen in JSON alongside the public HTML companion; the 3D ethylene regional study is a sibling page (same GitHub Pages tree).
