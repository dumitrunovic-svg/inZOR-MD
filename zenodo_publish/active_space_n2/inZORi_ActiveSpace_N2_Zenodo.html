inZOR-ND Active Space Selection: Bio-Adaptive Discovery of Optimal CASSCF Active Spaces for N₂ Dissociation Without Chemical Priors

Dumitru Novic
Independent Researcher · dumitru.novic@gmail.com · March 2026
Abstract. We apply the inZOR-ND bio-adaptive discovery engine to the active space selection problem in strongly correlated quantum chemistry. For N₂ dissociation across three geometries (R = 1.1, 1.5, 2.0 Å) with a 6-31G basis set, inZOR-ND selects the optimal CASSCF(6,6) active space from C(18,6) = 18,564 possible choices — without any chemical priors, orbital symmetry labels, or occupation-number thresholds. inZOR-ND evaluates 541 active spaces (2.91%) of the total search space and discovers the global optimum, converged at all three geometries simultaneously. The bio-adaptive search dynamics naturally reveal 10 degenerate optimal spaces, symmetry-equivalent under N₂'s D∞h group — discovered with no symmetry information provided. 1. The Active Space Selection Problem

Multi-reference quantum chemistry methods such as CASSCF require selecting a subset of molecular orbitals (the active space) for explicit correlation treatment. For a molecule with n orbitals and target active space size k, there are C(n,k) possible choices. Current approaches rely on domain expertise, natural orbital occupation numbers, or iterative entropy-based procedures. All require significant chemical knowledge and fail for novel systems. inZOR-ND requires none of this.

2. inZOR-ND Methodology

2.1 Continuous-to-Discrete Mapping

inZOR-ND operates in an 18-dimensional continuous space [0,1]18, one dimension per molecular orbital. Candidate position → active space via top-k ranking: the 6 dimensions with highest values select the 6 active orbitals. This creates a differentiable, cache-friendly mapping from continuous adaptive dynamics to discrete orbital selection.

2.2 Fitness Function

Each evaluation runs CASSCF(6,6) at all three geometries simultaneously via PySCF:

fitness = −mean_E(CASSCF, converged_geom) − 0.003 × n_orb − 0.5 × n_unconverged

Multi-geometry aggregation forces inZOR-ND to find spaces that perform well along the full dissociation curve, not just at equilibrium — correctly weighting strongly correlated configurations near dissociation.

2.3 Parallelization — Prefetch Strategy

Before each adaptive step, a prefetch routine generates candidate positions, collects unique uncached masks, and evaluates them in a single batch using ProcessPoolExecutor with 14 workers (OMP_NUM_THREADS=1 per worker). Step 1 evaluates 78 CASSCF calculations in parallel; later steps exploit the cache (~97% hit rate by step 50).

3. Results

3.1 Optimal Active Space

inZOR-ND identifies the optimal CASSCF(6,6) space as MOs [2, 4, 5, 7, 11, 15] (0-indexed from HF calculation), corresponding to the σ(2s)/π(2p)/π*(2p)/σ*(2p) bonding manifold — the chemically expected space for N₂, recovered without any orbital character information.

4. Conclusions

  • inZOR-ND solves active space selection without any chemical knowledge — no symmetry labels, no occupation numbers, no expert input.
  • 97.1% reduction in CASSCF evaluations vs brute-force, finding the global optimum.
  • Natural degeneracy discovery — the bio-adaptive search spontaneously reveals all 10 symmetry-equivalent optimal spaces.
  • Multi-geometry fitness correctly weights strongly correlated geometries (correlation energy ×3 at R=2.0Å vs equilibrium).
  • Scalable to larger systems where brute-force is infeasible and domain expertise is contested (Cr₂, Fe-porphyrin).
  • The same inZOR-ND engine, unmodified, that solved QEC on IBM Quantum also solves active space selection in quantum chemistry.
