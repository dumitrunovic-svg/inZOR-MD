inZOR-ND: Hardware-Native Quantum Error Correction Discovered by Genomic Evolution on IBM Quantum (7-qubit, Heron Processor) — v1 + v2

Novic, Dumitru
Independent Researcher
March 2026 · Preprint v2 · inZOR-ND Research Series
Abstract We apply the inZOR-ND bio-adaptive genomic discovery engine to quantum error correction (QEC) on real IBM Quantum hardware, without any prior knowledge of QEC theory. Two versions are presented: v1 (21 Ry parameters, X-only correction with 8 syndromes, 4 test states) and v2 (42 Ry+Rz parameters, Full Pauli correction with 22 syndromes, 6 test states, multi-noise training over 4 noise levels). Both versions discover hardware-native 7-qubit encoding circuits optimized for the IBM Heron processor's tree topology. Across 7 hardware runs on ibm_fez and ibm_marrakesh, inZOR-ND beats Steane [[7,1,3]] in every single run, with gains from +0.2 pp to +5.1 pp. The v1 LUPA precision zoom achieves simulation fidelity 0.999843 (p_cz=0.002), while v2 reaches 0.990244 (multi-noise average, Full Pauli). v2 Qiskit cross-validation confirms perfect match (diff < 1e-16).

1. Introduction

Quantum error correction (QEC) is essential for fault-tolerant quantum computing. The Steane [[7,1,3]] code is the theoretical benchmark for 7 physical qubits. However, IBM Heron processors impose specific constraints: heavy-hex lattice connectivity, native CZ/Rz/SX gate set (CNOT requires decomposition), and ~0.2% error rate per CZ gate.

We ask: can inZOR-ND discover a QEC code better suited to the actual hardware, without any QEC theory? We present two versions with progressively stricter evaluation criteria, validated on 7 real hardware runs.

8. Limitations

  • In ideal simulation, Steane with Full Pauli correction outperforms inZOR-ND; advantage is hardware-specific
  • Hardware results sensitive to calibration — 7 runs is moderate but not exhaustive
  • The discovered codes have not been formally characterized (distance, stabilizer group)
  • v2 did not use LUPA; additional refinement could improve v2 simulation fidelity
  • Only tested on IBM Heron topology; generalization to other architectures untested

9. Conclusions

inZOR-ND beats Steane [[7,1,3]] in 7/7 IBM hardware runs
v1: +5.1 pp (noisy) · v2: +1.5 pp (ibm_fez) · Hardware-native design outperforms theoretical optimality

inZOR-ND demonstrates that a bio-adaptive engine with zero quantum domain knowledge can discover competitive QEC codes by exploiting hardware-native gate sets. The key insight: shallower native circuits accumulate less decoherence than theoretically optimal but deeper circuits.
